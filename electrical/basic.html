Ohm's Law

Ohm's Law describes the relationship between voltage (V), current (I), and resistance (R). It states that the current flowing through a conductor between two points is directly proportional to the voltage across the two points and inversely proportional to the resistance between them. The formula is given by:

V = I × R

Resistor

A resistor is an electrical component that limits or regulates the flow of electrical current in an electronic circuit. Resistors can also be used to provide a specific voltage for an active device such as a transistor. They are one of the most common components used in electronic circuits. Resistors come in a variety of shapes and sizes, but they all function in the same basic way. The value of a resistor is measured in ohms (Ω) and is usually indicated by a color code on the body of the resistor.

Use the color bands on a resistor to determine its resistance value. Select the colors for each band to calculate the resistance.

Inductor

An inductor is a passive electronic component that stores energy in a magnetic field when current flows through it.

The formula for the voltage across an inductor is V = L * dI/dt.

Capacitor

A capacitor is a passive electronic component that stores electrical energy in an electric field.

Formula: Q = C * V

Joule's Law

Joule's Law describes the relationship between power dissipation, current, and resistance in a resistor.
