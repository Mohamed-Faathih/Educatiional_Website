Ohm's Law

Ohm's Law describes the relationship between voltage (V), current (I), and resistance (R). It states that the current flowing through a conductor between two points is directly proportional to the voltage across the two points and inversely proportional to the resistance between them. The formula is given by:

\(V = I \times R\)

Resistor

A resistor is an electrical component that limits or regulates the flow of electrical current in an electronic circuit. Resistors can also be used to provide a specific voltage for an active device such as a transistor. They are one of the most common components used in electronic circuits. Resistors come in a variety of shapes and sizes, but they all function in the same basic way. The value of a resistor is measured in ohms (Œ©) and is usually indicated by a color code on the body of the resistor.

Use the color bands on a resistor to determine its resistance value. Select the colors for each band to calculate the resistance.

Inductor

An inductor is a passive electronic component that stores energy in a magnetic field when current flows through it.

The formula for the voltage across an inductor is \(V = L \times \frac {dI}{dt}\).

Capacitor

A capacitor is a passive electronic component that stores electrical energy in an electric field.

Formula: Q = \(C \times V\)

Form Factor

Form factor is the ratio of the RMS value to the average value of an alternating current waveform.

Formula:

\( \text{Form Factor} = \frac{I_{\text{RMS}}}{I_{\text{average}}} \)

Peak Factor

Peak factor is the ratio of the peak value to the RMS value of a waveform.

Formula:

\( \text{Peak Factor} = \frac{I_{\text{peak}}}{I_{\text{RMS}}} \)

Crest Factor

Crest factor is the ratio of the peak value to the RMS value of a waveform, similar to the peak factor but used in a different context.

Formula:

\( \text{Crest Factor} = \frac{I_{\text{peak}}}{I_{\text{RMS}}} \)

Difference Between Magnetic and Electric Fields

Magnetic fields and electric fields are fundamental aspects of electromagnetism, but they have different properties and effects.

  • Electric Field: Created by electric charges. Its strength is given by E = Fq.
  • Magnetic Field: Created by moving charges (current). Its strength is given by B = Fqv.
